Projects

Portable Non-Enzymatic Electrochemical Glucose Detection System

Final Year Research Project

The project focused on designing a stable and enzyme-free glucose sensor using nanostructured metal oxides with high electrocatalytic activity. A three electrode system was integrated with a custom-built potentiostat circuit and ESP32 microcontroller for wireless data acquisition. Electrochemical techniques such as cyclic voltammetry (CV) and chronoamperometry (CA) were applied to analyze glucose concentrations, while a mobile application was developed for real-time monitoring. This research provided me with hands-on experience in nanomaterials, electrochemical sensor design, circuit development, and IoT-based health monitoring systems.

Used Technologies: ESP32 | Arduino IDE | Android Studio | Proteus | Origin
